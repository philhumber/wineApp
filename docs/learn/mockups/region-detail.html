Burgundy

Bourgogne, France

The spiritual home of Pinot Noir and Chardonnay. Burgundy is where terroir became a religion -- where two vineyards separated by a footpath can produce wines of entirely different character. The Climats of Burgundy are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Terroir
🌎
Soil
Limestone and clay marl. The famous "Kimmeridgian" limestone of Chablis contains ancient oyster fossils from a prehistoric seabed.
🌤
Climate
Cool continental with warm summers. Frost risk in spring. The marginal climate is what gives Burgundy its ethereal delicacy.
⛰
Geography
East-facing slopes (cotes) that catch morning sun. Altitude: 200-400m. The gentle hillsides drain perfectly for vine health.
