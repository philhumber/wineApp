The Fog Grape of Piedmont
Nebbiolo gets its name from "nebbia" -- the autumn fog that blankets Piedmont's vineyards as the grape reaches late ripeness. Despite producing some of Italy's most powerful wines, Nebbiolo is surprisingly pale in the glass.
